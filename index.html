Dr. Ashish Kumar

Associate Professor of Physics

Department of Physics & Astronomical Sciences
Central University of Jammu, J&K — 181143, India

Experimental condensed matter physicist specialising in defect physics and radiation–matter interactions in wide-bandgap semiconductors, ion-beam modification of functional materials, and thermoelectric energy conversion. His laboratory investigates how energetic heavy-ion irradiation modifies the electronic structure and defect landscape of GaN, SiC, and ZnO — research with direct relevance to radiation-tolerant electronics for space and nuclear environments. The group has developed one of India’s rare in-situ DLTS facilities for real-time defect characterisation under live swift heavy-ion beams, supported continuously by ISRO, DRDO, and IUAC.

About

Probing Matter at the Quantum Scale

Dr. Ashish Kumar is an experimental physicist whose work bridges fundamental condensed matter physics and applied energy technologies. He completed his Ph.D. from IIT Delhi in 2013 under Prof. Rajendra Singh, pioneering in-situ electrical characterisation of GaN devices under live ion-beam irradiation.

His decade-long tenure at the Inter-University Accelerator Centre (IUAC), New Delhi — first as a DST-INSPIRE Faculty awardee and later as a SERB Research Scientist — saw him independently design five custom cryogenic measurement systems and publish 30+ papers. He is now Associate Professor at Central University of Jammu.

His research programme spans thermoelectric energy harvesting, wide-bandgap semiconductor physics, defect engineering via ion beams, 2D materials, and space instrumentation — united by a passion for building indigenous scientific infrastructure.

Indigenous Instrumentation

Custom-Built Scientific Systems

Five precision measurement systems independently designed, fabricated, and commissioned — a defining feature of the research programme

System I · Published Rev. Sci. Instrum.

Seebeck & Resistivity Setup

80 K – 650 K · Wire, thin film & bulk. One of very few such systems designed indigenously in India. Used by multiple collaborating groups nationally.

System II · Unique in Indian Universities

Thermal Conductivity & ZT Setup

300 K – 1000 K · Complete thermoelectric figure-of-merit (ZT) characterisation. Rare capability at Indian universities.
